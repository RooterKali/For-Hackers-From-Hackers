# Contributing to For Hackers, From Hackers

Thank you for helping improve the knowledge base.

## What You Can Contribute

You can suggest:

- Bug bounty tips and methodologies
- Security research and technical write-ups
- Useful open-source security tools
- Browser extensions for security testing
- Wordlists and content discovery resources
- Bug bounty dorks
- CTF and learning resources
- AI security research
- Other high-quality resources relevant to bug bounty hunters and security researchers

## How to Contribute

### Option 1: Open an Issue

Open a GitHub Issue with:

- Resource name or title
- Link to the original source
- Original author/researcher, when known
- Category
- A short explanation of why it is useful

### Option 2: Submit a Pull Request

If the contribution changes files in this repository, fork the repository, make your changes, and submit a Pull Request with a clear description.

## Contribution Guidelines

- Credit original researchers and creators whenever possible.
- Link to the original source rather than copying entire articles or research without permission.
- Do not submit leaked credentials, private data, stolen data, malware, or unauthorized confidential information.
- Do not submit resources whose primary purpose is malicious or unauthorized activity.
- Keep descriptions concise and useful.
- Avoid duplicate submissions.

## Review

Submissions may be reviewed, edited, categorized, or declined to maintain the quality and organization of the knowledge base.

By contributing, you agree that your contribution may be referenced or incorporated into the public **For Hackers, From Hackers** knowledge base.
